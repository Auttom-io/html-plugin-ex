3-Day Christmas Itinerary in Albania with A.S KASALLA - Rental Cars

Feel restful in the country of hospitality and tradition.

Day 1: Tirana – A Festive Capital

Morning

• Arrive in Tirana, Albania’s lively capital, beautifully decorated for Christmas.

• Stroll through Skanderbeg Square, transformed into a Christmas market with food stalls, crafts, and a sparkling Christmas tree.

Afternoon

• Visit Blloku, a trendy area known for cafes and restaurants, perfect for a festive lunch.

• Head to the National History Museum to dive into Albania’s cultural heritage.

Evening

• Take a ride on the Dajti Ekspres Cable Car for panoramic views of the city and surrounding snow-dusted mountains.

• Enjoy a cozy dinner at a traditional restaurant like Era Vila.

Driving Tip
No driving required on Day 1, but pick up your rental car in the evening to be ready for the next day.

Day 2: Berat – A Fairytale Christmas Town

Morning

• Drive ~2 hours to Berat, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that feels magical at Christmas.

• Explore the Kala (Castle) for panoramic views of the city and surrounding hills.

• Visit the Onufri Museum, housed in a centuries-old church.

Afternoon

• Wander through the festive streets of Mangalem and Gorica, Berat’s historic neighborhoods known for their Ottoman-era architecture.

• Enjoy a traditional Albanian Christmas lunch at Restaurant Mangalemi.

Evening

• Stay overnight in a boutique guesthouse like Hotel Muzaka, which offers both comfort and authentic charm.

Driving Tip
The roads to Berat are generally well-maintained, but be cautious of possible winter fog.

Day 3: Kruja and Durres – A Historical and Coastal Finale

Morning

• Drive ~ 2 hours to Kruja, a historic town nestled in the mountains.

• Visit the Kruja Castle and the Skanderbeg Museum, dedicated to Albania’s national hero.

• Explore the Old Bazaar, perfect for picking up handcrafted Christmas gifts.

Afternoon

• Continue driving ~1 hour to Durres, Albania’s coastal city.

• Walk along the seaside promenade and visit the Durres Amphitheater, one of the largest Roman ruins in the Balkans.

• Enjoy a seafood lunch with views of the Adriatic Sea at Vertigo Restaurant.

Evening

• Return to Tirana (~40 minutes) for your departure or an optional festive dinner to conclude your trip.

Driving Tip
The roads between Kruja, Durres, and Tirana are well-paved, making for an easy drive even in winter.

Find your suitable car at A.S KASALLA during Christmas

Vehicle Recommendation:

• A compact car works well for city-to-city travel.

• Choose an SUV if you plan to visit mountainous regions.

Winter Features:

• Ensure your rental car has winter tires and a functioning heating system

• Snow chains may be necessary for mountain roads.

Booking Advice:

• Reserve early as Christmas can see a slight increase in demand for rentals.

• Opt for full insurance coverage to avoid stress on icy or wet roads.

Fuel Policy:

• Fill up in larger towns and cities, as smaller villages may have limited gas stations open during holidays.

Winter Driving Tips

• Check Weather Conditions: Be aware of possible fog, rain, or snow, especially in northern or higher altitude areas.

• Daylight Hours: Days are shorter in December, so plan to drive during daylight for safer and more enjoyable travel.

• Navigation: Use GPS or offline maps like Google Maps or Maps.me, as signage can be limited in rural areas.

What to Pack for a Christmas Trip in Albania?

• Warm layers and a winter coat for chilly evenings;

• Comfortable walking shoes for cobblestone streets in Berat and Kruja.

• Power bank and travel charger for devices.

• A reusable water bottle and snacks for the road.
